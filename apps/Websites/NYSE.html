Today’s Stock Market

Michael Reinking, CFA

October 13, 2025 at 1:15 p.m. EST

What a difference a weekend makes. There is some optimism today with equity markets recouping about half of Friday’s selloff. Over the weekend the administration toned down some of the commentary around China while Beijing suggested there could be “exemptions” to the announced restrictions. This morning Treasury Secretary Bessent said the two sides had communications over the weekend, that there will be staff level meetings this week and he still believes that the meeting between President Trump/Xi scheduled for later this month will still happen. The other event on a global scale was Hamas releasing the remaining hostages after two years a main as part of the recently negotiated cease fire agreement. Back in the US, the government remains shutdown with speculation that this could drag on for weeks as the administration pulled levers to ensure the military is paid this week removing a point of leverage which could have served to force negotiations. One side note, the BLS will publish the CPI data on October 24th as this is used to calculate the annual Social Security cost of living adjustment which is published on November 1. And like the sun rises, there was another partnership announced by OpenAI, just ahead of the open, this time with Broadcom (+~10%) to deploy 10gw of AI accelerators. Keep in mind today is also the start of Oracle’s AI World conference.
